# Design Context

## Users

**Primary audience:** Potential employers, design directors, VPs of Product evaluating Jed's expertise and deciding whether to hire. Talent scouts at companies building at the physical-digital intersection.

**Their context:** Quickly scanning portfolios to assess range, taste, and design thinking. They have seen hundreds of portfolios. Looking for signal, not noise — specifically for someone who understands both physical product and software.

**Job to be done:** Determine with confidence that Jed Crystal has the expertise, range, and intentionality they need.

**Emotional goal:** Leave with a specific memory of this portfolio. Confidence through demonstrated taste. A sense of talking to someone who has *built and shipped real things* — not a student project or spec work collection.

---

## Brand Personality

**Three words:** Precise. Curious. Grounded.

**Voice & tone:** Professional but not corporate. Specific. No hedging. Every sentence earns its place. The portfolio of someone who has done things, not just designed for them.

**Emotional direction:** Quietly confident. The design should feel purposeful and alive without being loud. Restraint that demonstrates mastery — like a well-made physical object: you notice the quality without being told to.

**What this is NOT:** Flashy or attention-seeking. Template-generated. Safe or forgettable. The AI dark mode palette (cyan glows, magenta accents, purple-to-blue gradients). Busy or complex for its own sake. Glitch aesthetics. Heavy glow effects.

---

## Aesthetic Direction

**Visual tone:** Dark mode only — intentional design choice, not a user preference. Technical precision meets craftsmanship. Industrial without being cold.

**References:**
- zerovector.design — admired for technical character, dramatic spacing, and animation sophistication
- open.zerovector.design — admired for clarity and information hierarchy
- Direction here is *less busy*: one accent color, cleaner component language, more breathing room, no multi-color glow scheme

**Anti-references:** Heavy glow effects, glitch/CRT aesthetics, multi-color accent schemes, glassmorphism, generic card grids, safe minimal that's just empty.

**Color palette:**
- Background: `#0E0E0F` — cool-tinted dark, not pure black
- Surface (elevated): `#141416` — slightly lifted backgrounds
- Primary text: `#F2F2F0` — near white, very slightly warm
- Secondary text: `#888894` — cool grey
- Tertiary / borders: `#1E1E24` — subtle cool-tinted boundary
- **Accent: `#F5631A`** — safety orange, industrial, distinctive, used sparingly
- No secondary accent colors. Orange is the only color that earns emphasis.

**Typography:**
- Display (h1, hero, large titles): **Syne** — geometric, distinctive at large sizes, free on Google Fonts (weights: 600, 700, 800)
- Body + UI + secondary headings: **Space Grotesk** — already loaded, geometric, legible (weights: 300, 400, 600, 700)
- No monospace. No serif. No Inter, Poppins, Roboto, or Montserrat.
- Note: Syne must be added to index.html Google Fonts link alongside Space Grotesk

**Icons:**
- Custom inline SVG only — no icon libraries
- Style: geometric and precise, single-stroke, no fill
- Aesthetic: technical drawing / industrial diagram — like something from a CAD callout or engineering specification
- Icons earn their presence — used to orient, not decorate

**Layout:**
- Max content width: 960px (existing constraint, preserved)
- Dramatic vertical section spacing — cathedral-like breathing room
- Left-aligned content as default; asymmetric compositions where intentional
- No rounded cards with drop shadows. Borders (`#1E1E24`) or whitespace as separators only.
- Headshot photo allowed on About page (Option C)

**Animation:**
- Entrance animations with staggered reveals (Intersection Observer for scroll-driven)
- Hover interactions: precise and subtle — translate, color shift, opacity
- Easing: exponential `cubic-bezier(0.16, 1, 0.3, 1)` throughout
- All animations respect `prefers-reduced-motion: reduce`
- No bounce, no elastic easing, no spring-for-its-own-sake
- Nothing moves just to move — every animation has a reason

## Design Principles

1. **Industrial precision**
   Every element placed deliberately. Tight control of spacing, alignment, and proportion. The design should feel engineered, not decorated. When in doubt, subtract.

2. **Restraint as signal**
   The orange accent appears once per view, maximum. If it appears more, it's decoration. Restraint is how we communicate that a designer — not a template — made this.

3. **Memorable through one unexpected thing**
   One considered detail per section — a typographic treatment, a micro-interaction, a compositional choice — that makes a hiring manager pause. Not gimmicks. Decisions that reveal taste.

4. **Motion with purpose**
   Entrance animations confirm load. Hover states confirm interactivity. Scroll reveals reward attention. Nothing else moves. The test: if you removed the animation, would something be missing? If not, remove it.

5. **Physical-digital credibility**
   The design language should reflect the portfolio's subject matter. Precision, materiality, and craft — not the aesthetic of a purely digital-native designer. The feel of a well-made object.

## Code Conventions

- React functional components only, plain CSS (no Tailwind, no CSS-in-JS)
- All colors and spacing via CSS variables in `src/index.css`
- Never use inline styles (`style={{}}` prohibited)
- Custom SVG icons inline in JSX — no external icon libraries
- One page component per file in `src/pages/`, each with its own `.css`
- Shared/global CSS in `src/App.css`
- Animations defined in CSS only — not JavaScript
- Headshot photo allowed on About page
- 960px max-width container — do not change
- Dark mode fixed — no theme toggle, ever
